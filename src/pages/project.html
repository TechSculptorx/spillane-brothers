PROJECT

Cork University Hospital, Emergency Department

Extension to and internal re-alignment of the Emergency Department at Cork University Hospital.

PROJECT DETAILS

This project was completed on an emergency basis in response to the covid-19 pandemic. The purpose of the project was to provide a building to enlarge the Emergency Department to better manage the flow of people from entry, through triage to socially distanced waiting and treatment. This required the new building to be built at the existing entrance to the ED on a fast track programme.

To maintain the full operation of emergency services, the project was split up into the following phases

1

PHASE ONE

Works to facilitate the re-routing of roadways, set down areas and access/egress routes into the Emergency Department.

2

PHASE TWO

Introduce glazed walls and seating pods in the existing foyer to create safe waiting spaces & new circulation routes. This was done while it was still being used as the entrance & waiting area for the Emergency Department – during wave 2 of the covid-19 pandemic.

3

PHASE THREE

Construct the new building, on a fast track programme, on a very restricted site, including all site retaining walls to deal with level variations, paving etc. The building has all of the operating systems of a state of the art, fully functional Emergency Department.

4

PHASE FOUR

Re-alignment & re-purposing of the old reception and waiting area in the existing ED.

The Emergency Department remained operating at full capacity throughout.

We are very proud of this project and the team that worked weekends and 12-15 hour days for months to get it into operation as quickly as it was possible to do so.
